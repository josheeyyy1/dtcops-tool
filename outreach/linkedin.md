# LinkedIn Messages

LinkedIn is the warm channel for the first 15 (your network) and the second
touch for cold prospects. Profile first: before sending anything, your
headline should read close to the positioning line, e.g. "Ops automation for
supplement brands selling across UK, EU and US". People check the profile
before replying; the profile must confirm the message.

## Connection request note (cold, 300-char limit)

> Ran ops at a supplements brand across UK/EU/US, now I build stock and
> reorder systems for other supplement brands. Your {{observation, short}}
> caught my eye. Keen to connect with people running multi-region DTC ops.

No ask in the connection note. The ask comes after they accept.

## After they accept (or first message to existing connections)

> Thanks for connecting, {{first name}}.
>
> Short version of why I reached out: {{observation}}. I ran ops in-house at
> a supplements brand shipping UK, EU and US. The thing that nearly broke us
> was stock split across warehouses: three systems, three different numbers,
> reorders by gut feel. I built the fix in-house and now do it for other
> supplement brands.
>
> Would a 30-minute call comparing notes on how you run stock across regions
> be useful? No pitch. If your setup's solid I'll say so and that's that.

## Existing network (the first 15)

Skip the observation hunt; be direct about what's changed:

> {{First name}}, quick one. Since we last spoke I've gone out on my own:
> I build stock and reorder systems for supplement brands selling across
> regions, same thing I built at {{brand}}. I'm doing free 30-minute scoping
> calls while I sharpen the offer. Fancy one, or know a founder drowning in
> stock spreadsheets who would?

The referral ask at the end matters: your first 15 know your work ethic even
if they don't own a supplement brand. A warm intro beats ten cold emails.
