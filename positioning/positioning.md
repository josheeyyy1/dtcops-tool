# Positioning and ICP

## The wedge

**DTC supplement and wellness brands doing £1–10m on Shopify, selling across
two or more regions (UK/EU/US), where stock lives in more than one warehouse
and reordering is still done by feel.**

Your suggested wedge was right. I sharpened it on one axis: **multi-region is
the qualifier, not just the niche**. Here's why, from your own background:

- Your hardest-won, least-copyable experience is UK/EU/US stock across
  multiple 3PLs: one source of truth, sync, reorder logic across regions.
  Plenty of people can set up a reorder point for one warehouse. Very few have
  run it across three regions with different lead times, MOQs and 3PLs.
- Single-region brands can limp along on a spreadsheet or a Shopify app.
  Multi-region brands can't: the moment stock splits across warehouses,
  every off-the-shelf tool gets awkward and every founder starts doing manual
  reconciliation. That's acute pain, not mild pain. Acute pain buys audits.
- Supplements make it worse in exactly the ways you've already solved:
  expiry dates, batch/lot tracking pressure, repeat-purchase velocity that
  punishes stock-outs harder than one-off products, and cash tied up in
  long-lead-time manufacturing runs.

So: supplements is the niche, £1–10m Shopify is the size, **split stock across
regions is the knife**.

## One-line positioning

Primary (site header, LinkedIn bio):

> I build the ops backbone for supplement brands selling across UK, EU and US:
> one accurate view of stock in every warehouse, reorder points that fire
> before you run out, and the spreadsheet work automated away.

Short version (cold email, verbal):

> I help supplement brands with stock in more than one warehouse stop running
> out, stop over-ordering, and stop reconciling spreadsheets by hand.

## ICP (ideal client profile)

| Dimension | Target | Signal to look for |
|---|---|---|
| Category | Supplements, vitamins, wellness consumables | Repeat-purchase product, expiry-dated stock |
| Revenue | £1–10m/yr | 8–40 staff, founder still close to ops |
| Platform | Shopify (incl. Plus) | Storefront tech is visible from the site |
| Fulfilment | 2+ regions or 2+ warehouses/3PLs | Ships UK+EU or UK+US, separate EU entity, "our US 3PL" language |
| Ops tooling | Spreadsheets + inbox, or a half-used inventory app | No ops hire, or one overloaded ops person |
| Pain right now | Stock-outs on hero SKUs, over-ordering, month-end stock reconciliation taking days | Sold-out badges on bestsellers, restock waitlists, "back in stock" emails |
| Buyer | Founder or Head of Ops | Person who personally feels the 10pm spreadsheet session |

## Disqualifiers (do not pursue)

- Single warehouse, single region, no expansion planned: pain isn't acute enough.
- Under £1m: can't fund the build; over £10m: they need an ERP and an ops team, not me.
- Already on a full ERP (NetSuite, Unleashed used properly): the problem is solved or political.
- Wants a custom app, a warehouse, or fulfilment renegotiation: not what I sell.
- Marketplace-first (Amazon-only): different stock model, different tooling.
