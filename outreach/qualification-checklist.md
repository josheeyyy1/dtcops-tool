# Qualification Checklist: who belongs on the first-15 list

**DECISION for Joshua: you supply the 15 names.** This checklist decides
who makes the cut. List 30 candidates from LinkedIn connections, old
colleagues, suppliers, 3PL contacts, agency people, trade-event founders —
score them, send to the top 15.

## Hard gates (any "no" = off the list, however friendly)

| Gate | Pass |
|---|---|
| G1. Can they buy, or introduce you to someone who can? | Founder, co-founder, MD, Head of Ops, or one warm intro away |
| G2. Consumables/repeat-purchase DTC? | Supplements ideal; skincare, food/pet supplements count |
| G3. Roughly £1–10m revenue? | Estimate from team size (8–40), funding news, or your own knowledge |
| G4. On Shopify? | Check the storefront source, 10 seconds |
| G5. Would they take your call this month? | If not, they're cold outreach, not first-15 |

## Scoring the survivors (0–2 each, top 15 get sent)

| Signal | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1. Multi-region stock | Single warehouse, no plans | Expanding within 12 months | 2+ warehouses/3PLs now |
| S2. Visible stock pain | None visible | Occasional sold-out badges | Restock waitlists, founder moaning about ops publicly |
| S3. Ops capacity | Solid ops team | One overloaded ops person | Founder does ops personally |
| S4. Relationship strength | Would need reminding who you are | Warm, dormant | Has seen your work first-hand |
| S5. Referral value | Knows nobody relevant | Some DTC network | Investor, agency owner, 3PL insider, podcast host |

**Interpretation:**

- **8–10:** first 5 to contact, Monday, personally.
- **5–7:** the middle 10 — template plus real personalisation.
- **Under 5 but S5=2:** contact anyway, as a referral ask, not a prospect.
  "Who do you know" beats "do you want" here.
- **Under 5 otherwise:** cold list for week 2+, not the first 15.

## Places to mine your network

Old employer's suppliers, co-manufacturers, 3PL account managers, freight
forwarders, packaging suppliers, the Shopify/email agencies, fellow ops
leads from communities, ex-colleagues at other brands, trade-show founders,
LinkedIn commenters on ops posts, an old investor, podcast guests, DTC
operator Slack/Facebook groups, and anyone who ever asked "how did you sort
your inventory?" That last group goes to the top.
