# Cold Email

Rules: under 110 words. One observable fact about them, one credibility line,
one question. No links in the first email (deliverability and it reads less
like a campaign). Send from your normal address, plain text, no signature
block beyond your name. Send Tuesday to Thursday, 8:30 to 10:00 their time.

The `{{observation}}` is the work. It must be something you actually saw:
a sold-out badge, a restock waitlist, a "back in stock" email you signed up
for, a job ad for an ops role, a podcast mention of US expansion. Never
"I came across your brand".

---

## Primary template

**Subject:** `{{SKU or product name}} out of stock`
*(or: `stock across {{region 1}} and {{region 2}}`)*

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> {{Observation. One sentence. Example shape: "Signed up for your restock
> alert on [product] a couple of weeks back and it's still showing sold out
> in the UK store."}}
>
> I ask because I ran ops at a supplements brand shipping UK, EU and US, and
> our version of that problem was stock sitting in the wrong region while
> the selling region ran dry. Reordering by gut feel across warehouses was
> the root cause. I fixed it in-house and now do the same thing for other
> supplement brands.
>
> Worth a 30-minute call to compare notes on how you run stock across
> regions? No pitch, and if your setup's already solid I'll say so.
>
> Joshua

## Variant: no visible stock-out (use ops-hire or expansion signal)

**Subject:** `your {{ops role}} ad` / `US launch`

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> Saw {{observation: the ops hire ad / the US 3PL announcement / the EU
> store launch}}.
>
> When I ran ops at a supplements brand across UK, EU and US, that stage,
> {{second warehouse / first ops hire}}, was exactly when our spreadsheets
> stopped coping: three stock numbers that never agreed and reorders by gut
> feel. I built the system that fixed it and now deploy it for other
> supplement brands.
>
> Open to a 30-minute call on how you're handling stock across regions?
> Not a pitch. If what you have works, I'll tell you.
>
> Joshua

## Why it's built this way

- Subject is specific and lowercase: reads like a colleague, not a campaign.
- The credibility is "I had your job and fixed this", not client logos you
  don't have yet. True and stronger than logos at this stage.
- The ask is a conversation, and the "I'll say so" line is the honesty
  signal that gets replies from founders who ignore agencies.
- No em dashes, no exclamation marks, no "hope you're well".
