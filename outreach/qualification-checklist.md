# Qualification Checklist: who belongs on the first-15 list

**DECISION for Joshua: you supply the 15 names. This checklist decides who
makes the cut.** Work through your LinkedIn connections, old colleagues,
suppliers, 3PL contacts, agency people, and founders you've met at trade
events. List 30 candidates, score them here, send to the top 15.

## Hard gates (any "no" = off the list, however friendly they are)

| Gate | Pass |
|---|---|
| G1. Can they buy, or introduce you to someone who can? | Founder, co-founder, MD, Head of Ops, or one warm intro away |
| G2. Consumables/repeat-purchase DTC? | Supplements ideal; adjacent counts (skincare, food supplements, pet supplements) |
| G3. Roughly £1–10m revenue? | Estimate from team size (8–40), funding news, or your own knowledge |
| G4. On Shopify? | Check the storefront source, takes 10 seconds |
| G5. Would they take your call this month? | You'd bet a pint they reply. If not, they're cold outreach, not first-15 |

## Scoring the survivors (0–2 each, send to the top 15)

| Signal | 0 | 1 | 2 |
|---|---|---|---|
| S1. Multi-region stock | Single warehouse, no plans | Expanding within 12 months | 2+ warehouses/3PLs now |
| S2. Visible stock pain | None visible | Occasional sold-out badges | Restock waitlists, "back in stock" emails, founder moaning about ops publicly |
| S3. Ops capacity | Solid ops team | One overloaded ops person | Founder does ops personally |
| S4. Relationship strength | They'd need reminding who you are | Warm, dormant | They've seen your work first-hand |
| S5. Referral value | Knows nobody relevant | Some DTC network | Investor, agency owner, 3PL insider, podcast host: a node |

**Interpretation:**
- **8–10:** first 5 to contact, Monday, personally, no template smell.
- **5–7:** the middle 10. Template plus real personalisation.
- **Under 5 but S5 = 2:** contact them anyway, but as a referral ask, not a
  prospect. "Who do you know" beats "do you want" for these.
- **Under 5 otherwise:** cold list for week 2+, not the first 15.

## Fifteen places to mine your network

Old employer's: suppliers, co-manufacturers, 3PL account managers, freight
forwarders, packaging suppliers, the Shopify agency, the email agency,
fellow ops leads you met in communities, ex-colleagues who moved to other
brands, founders from trade shows, LinkedIn people who commented on ops
posts, the investor who looked at your old brand, industry podcast guests,
Facebook/Slack DTC operator groups, and anyone who ever asked "how did you
sort your inventory?"

That last group goes straight to the top of the list.
