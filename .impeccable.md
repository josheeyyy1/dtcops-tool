# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Founders and ops leads of DTC physical-product brands on Shopify (£1-10m revenue),
with stock split across UK/EU/US warehouses. They arrive from cold email or LinkedIn,
sceptical, busy, and burnt by agency hype. They are evaluating one person's competence
before spending £2,000 on an audit. Viewing context: desktop during the workday, phone
in the evening. Job to be done: "convince me you can bring order to my inventory chaos,
without wasting my time."

### Brand Personality
Precise, calm, engineered. British plainness: no hype, no em dashes in client-facing
copy, no invented clients/metrics/logos/testimonials. The site should feel like the
product being sold: a quiet, reliable system that just works. Emotional goals:
confidence and relief, not excitement.

### Aesthetic Direction
High-end premium minimalist high-tech, aligned with airtable.com's design language:
light theme, warm-white surfaces, near-black ink, one confident blue accent, generous
whitespace, soft depth, crisp grotesque type, data rendered honestly (tables, status
pills, tabular numerals). Anti-references: crypto-dark neon dashboards, gradient-text
startup slop, generic agency templates, stock photography.

Typography: Bricolage Grotesque (display/headings), Hanken Grotesk (body),
Spline Sans Mono (data/numbers only, tabular). Colors specified in OKLCH; neutrals
tinted toward the brand blue hue.

### Design Principles
1. The site is a demo of the service: every visual should feel like a calm,
   accurate operations system (live data, reconciliation, order from chaos).
2. Motion states facts: animations show sync, convergence, and alerts firing;
   never decoration for its own sake. Always respect prefers-reduced-motion.
3. Whitespace is the luxury: fewer elements, larger type, more air.
4. One accent, spent sparingly: blue marks live data and primary actions only.
5. Copy is sacred: never alter pricing, claims, or voice; design around the words.

### Hard Constraints
- Static Next.js (App Router) build, deploys to Vercel, no heavy new dependencies.
- Placeholders look like [[DESCRIPTION]]; never invent real-sounding specifics.
