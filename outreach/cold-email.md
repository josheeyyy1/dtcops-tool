# Cold Email

Under 110 words, one observed fact about them, one credibility line, one
question. No links in the first email. Plain text, your normal address, no
signature block beyond your name. Send Tuesday–Thursday, 8:30–10:00 their
time.

`{{observation}}` is the work: a sold-out badge, a restock waitlist, a "back
in stock" email you signed up for, an ops job ad, a podcast mention of
expansion. Never "I came across your brand."

---

## Primary template

**Subject:** `{{SKU or product name}} out of stock` (or `stock across
{{region 1}} and {{region 2}}`)

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> {{Observation, one sentence. E.g. "Signed up for your restock alert on
> [product] a couple of weeks back and it's still showing sold out in the
> UK store."}}
>
> I ask because I ran ops at a supplements brand shipping UK, EU and US, and
> our version of that problem was stock sitting in the wrong region while
> the selling region ran dry. Reordering by gut feel across warehouses was
> the root cause. I fixed it in-house and now do the same for other
> supplement brands.
>
> Worth a 30-minute call to compare notes on how you run stock across
> regions? No pitch, and if your setup's already solid I'll say so.
>
> Joshua

## Variant: no visible stock-out (ops-hire or expansion signal)

**Subject:** `your {{ops role}} ad` / `US launch`

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> Saw {{observation: the ops hire ad / the 3PL announcement / the store
> launch}}.
>
> When I ran ops at a supplements brand across UK, EU and US, that stage,
> {{second warehouse / first ops hire}}, was exactly when our spreadsheets
> stopped coping: three stock numbers that never agreed, reorders by gut
> feel. I built the fix and now deploy it for other supplement brands.
>
> Open to a 30-minute call on how you're handling stock across regions? Not
> a pitch. If what you have works, I'll tell you.
>
> Joshua

## Why it's built this way

Subject is specific and lowercase — reads like a colleague, not a campaign.
The credibility is "I had your job and fixed this," not client logos you
don't have yet. The ask is a conversation, and "I'll say so" is the honesty
signal that gets replies from founders who ignore agencies. No em dashes in
what you send — this file uses them only to mark editorial notes.
