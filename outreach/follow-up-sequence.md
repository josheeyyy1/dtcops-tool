# The 5-Touch Sequence

Cold prospects only (network contacts get one message and one nudge; don't
run sequences on people who know you). Stop the moment they reply, even to
say no. A "no" gets a one-line thanks and goes on the 90-day list.

Every touch must add something new. Never send "just bumping this".

| Touch | Day | Channel | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 0 | Email | The observation + credibility + soft ask (`cold-email.md`) |
| 2 | 3 | LinkedIn | Connection note (`linkedin.md`). Different channel, same face |
| 3 | 7 | Email | New angle: one concrete cost number |
| 4 | 14 | Email | Give something away: the self-serve checklist |
| 5 | 21 | Email | Close the loop, leave the door open |

## Touch 3 (day 7): the cost angle

**Subject:** re: {{original subject}}

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> One number from my time running supplement ops, in case it lands: a
> stock-out on a subscription SKU doesn't cost you one sale, it costs the
> subscriber. When we priced it honestly, every week our hero SKU was dark
> in a region cost more than a month of the fix.
>
> Still happy to compare notes on how you run stock across regions.
> 30 minutes, no pitch.
>
> Joshua

## Touch 4 (day 14): the giveaway

**Subject:** checklist, no call needed

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> Last useful thing from me, then I'll leave you alone. Eight questions I
> use to score a supplement brand's stock operation, the same ones I'd ask
> on a call:
>
> 1. Can you see accurate stock for every warehouse in one place?
> 2. Do reorder points come from velocity and lead time, or gut feel?
> 3. Does anyone check supplier lead times against what actually happened?
> 4. Has one region stocked out while another sat on months of cover?
> 5. Do you measure your 3PL's dispatch times, or trust them?
> 6. If two systems disagree on a stock number, which one wins?
> 7. How many hours a week does someone spend copying stock numbers around?
> 8. Do you know how much cash is in stock right now, and how much of it
>    expires within a year?
>
> Score a point per honest yes (question 4 scores on "no"). Under 5 means
> money is leaking. If you work through them and want a second pair of
> eyes, you know where I am.
>
> Joshua

This touch gets forwarded. It's the audit in miniature, which is the point:
anyone who self-scores 3/8 books the call themselves.

## Touch 5 (day 21): the close

**Subject:** closing the loop

> Hi {{first name}},
>
> I'll stop here. If stock across regions becomes the fire you have to put
> out, whether that's next quarter or after the next launch, my calendar
> link is below and the offer stands: 30 minutes, no pitch, and if your
> setup is solid I'll say so.
>
> Good luck with {{product / launch / the season}}.
>
> Joshua
>
> {{booking link}}

First time a link appears in the sequence. After this, they go on the 90-day
re-touch list with a note of where things stood.

## Bookkeeping (non-negotiable)

Track every prospect in `outreach/pipeline.csv`: touch dates, replies,
outcome. The kill/keep decision in the README runs on this file, so if it
isn't filled in, you can't know whether this business works.
